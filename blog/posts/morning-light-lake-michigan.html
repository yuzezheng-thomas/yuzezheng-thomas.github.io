TRAVEL JOURNAL · CHICAGO · 26 MAY 2026

Morning Light Along Lake Michigan

Some mornings begin with almost no subject at all: only water, weather, and the slow arrival of light. Lake Michigan is familiar, but the shoreline changes quickly when clouds begin to open.

I like photographing this kind of scene because the frame becomes less about a location and more about timing. The lake holds the color, the horizon keeps the composition steady, and small human details give the scale back to the image.
