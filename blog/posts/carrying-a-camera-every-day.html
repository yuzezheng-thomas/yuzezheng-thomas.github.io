Notes On Carrying A Camera Every Day

For everyday walks, the best camera setup is the one that disappears quickly. I prefer to keep the process simple: one body, one lens, and enough flexibility to respond to light without stopping the walk.

The goal is not to photograph everything. It is to stay available to a few details that might otherwise pass unnoticed: a reflection, a color contrast, a gesture, or a small alignment between architecture and movement.
